📝 Prompt
"Explain the difference between precision and recall in machine learning evaluation, and give a concrete example of when high precision matters more than high recall."
Knowledge / QA Round 4 of 8
A
Model A
Hidden until you vote

Precision and recall are two complementary metrics for evaluating classifiers, especially with imbalanced data.

Precision: Of all items I predicted positive, how many actually were? TP / (TP + FP)

Recall: Of all actual positives, how many did I catch? TP / (TP + FN)

High precision example: Email spam filtering. If your filter has low precision, legitimate emails end up in spam. Users prefer missed spam over blocked real emails.

VS
B
Model B
Hidden until you vote

Think of this with a doctor analogy — it makes the tradeoff intuitive.

In medical screening, you maximize recall — catch every sick patient, even if some false alarms get ruled out later. Missing a case is worse than an unnecessary follow-up test.

In search relevance, precision matters more — nobody wants 100 mixed results when they want 5 great ones. Quality beats quantity.

The F1 score is the harmonic mean of both — it balances the tradeoff when neither can be sacrificed entirely.

Not sure which metric to use?

All Metrics by Category

Summarization
Best for: document condensing, news summarization
ROUGE-L Primary
Measures Longest Common Subsequence overlap between generated and reference summaries. Fast and reference-based.
BERTScore Secondary
Uses contextual BERT embeddings for semantic similarity. Slower but captures meaning beyond exact wording.
Question Answering
Best for: SQuAD-style extraction, factual QA
Exact Match Primary
Binary score: 1 if prediction matches ground truth exactly (after normalization), 0 otherwise. Simple and strict.
Token F1 Secondary
Softer version — measures token-level overlap between predicted and gold answer. More forgiving than Exact Match.
All metrics in the guide are linked to the Eval Wizard — hover any metric in the wizard to see its definition here, or click "Why this metric?" to jump to its explanation.
